## Register

**Brand.** This site IS the product. A visitor's first impression is the booking decision — the design carries the voice of the artist, not just lists features.

## Product purpose

Julie Schatz Music is the booking site of Julie Schatz — NYC-based violinist, jazz pianist, DJ, and producer. The site converts wedding planners, couples, and corporate event organizers in NY/NJ/CT into booking inquiries for live performance, and secondarily exposes the production / sample-pack catalog to producers.

Primary conversion: a serious inquiry submitted through the form (signed agreement + 50% retainer to follow). Bookings start at $595 (NYC ceremony) and reach $3,200+ (Soul Shades duo, ceremony + cocktail).

## Users

1. **Engaged couples and their planners** — researching a wedding violinist 6–12 months before a Saturday in May–October. Reading on a phone, often late at night, comparing 3–5 vendors. Need to know: does Julie sound like the wedding I'm picturing, can she travel to my venue, what does it cost.
2. **Corporate event producers / private hosts** — booking galas, brand events, proposals. More transactional, more price-tolerant, want fast confirmation.
3. **Independent producers** — finding Julie via Splice / Roland coverage, looking for sample packs and production work. Smaller traffic but a real audience.

## Brand voice (three concrete words)

**Warm, refined, unhurried.** Not luxury-cold, not bridal-cute. The site should feel like the experience of being in the room when she plays — quiet authority, technical command worn lightly, hospitality.

## Aesthetic identity (already shipped — preserve)

- **Lane**: editorial, dark, atmospheric. Premium violet/black with ivory tones.
- **Type**: Playfair Display italic + Cormorant Garamond + Inter. *Note: this is the textbook reflex-reject editorial-typographic stack per the impeccable brand register. It is the brand's established identity, so identity-preservation overrides — do not rip it out as part of polish. Future redesigns can reconsider.*
- **Surface**: animated WebGL liquid-violet shader background (deep violet → indigo → black with sine-wave warping). Distinctive, not generic gradient mesh.
- **Hero treatment**: kinetic letter-stagger reveal of "JULIE / SCHATZ" + photo marquee + single CTA ("Inquiry"). Single dominant idea.
- **Section vocabulary**: editorial kickers (Inter, all-caps, track-spaced) with thin gradient rules + Playfair italic h2.
- **Color strategy**: Committed — saturated violet carries the surface, ivory provides contrast.

## Anti-references (things to NOT look like)

*[Confirm or add — these are inferred from the site's positioning]*

- **Generic Squarespace wedding-vendor templates**: cream + serif + stock-photo carousels. The whole genre.
- **The Knot / WeddingWire vendor profile aesthetic**: stars, badges, listing-grid uniformity.
- **Other NYC wedding violinist sites**: most are either dated (early-2010s WordPress) or follow a generic "elegance" template. Julie's site needs to read as an artist's site that also happens to take wedding bookings, not a wedding-vendor site that happens to have an artist.
- **Crypto/fintech dark-mode neon**: same dark palette superficially, very different mood.

## Strategic principles

- **Identity as artist first, vendor second.** Julie is a Roland artist with Splice features and Wu-Tang/Disney credits. The site leads with that — wedding bookings come through the strength of the artist identity, not in spite of it. Don't hide the producer/DJ/sample-pack work to look more "wedding-appropriate."
- **No fabrication, ever.** AggregateRating schema intentionally omitted. Testimonials are verbatim. This must be preserved through any polish.
- **Speed and craft are the differentiators.** Response within 24 hours, low-profile high-fidelity PA, custom arrangements with 6 weeks lead time. The site should feel as carefully produced as the performance.
- **The site is read on a phone.** Mobile is not a constraint — it's the primary surface.

## Out of scope for polish

- Changing fonts (identity-preservation).
- Changing the WebGL shader (distinctive identity element).
- Editing testimonial copy (real customer quotes).
- Changing the form schema or pricing.
